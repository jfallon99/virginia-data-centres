Virginia's Data Center Sprawl
MAR 2026

Virginia was supposed to hit 100% clean electricity by 2045.
Data centers may have killed that goal.

Virginia's status as the 'world's data center capital' is undergoing a frantic geographic expansion south along the I-95 corridor. This growth is increasingly at odds with the state's decarbonisation pathways. To meet the surging baseload demand, utilities are proposing a pivot back to fossil gas infrastructure and the life-extension of legacy coal units, a move that critics argue shifts the financial burden of the 'digital gold rush' directly onto residential rate-payers.

The energy transition cost

Dominion Energy "does not currently see a viable path towards full retirement of all carbon-emitting resources by 2045", specifically citing data centre demand. The utility's 2025 plan includes at least 1,447 megawatts (MW) of new fossil fuel generation, including a 944 MW gas plant near Richmond on the site of a former coal plant.

The Regulatory Pivot: SB 253 and the Cost-Shift/div>

The GS-5 Shift: Virginia’s General Assembly passed SB 253, authorizing the SCC to decouple data center infrastructure costs from the residential base. Starting January 2027, the new GS-5 rate class mandates 14-year contracts with stringent minimum demand charges—projected to save average households $5.52/month.
The Storage Gap: While new long-duration energy storage (LDES) mandates are now statutory, the execution gap remains vast. Only 8.94 MW of LDES is currently under construction, a negligible fraction of a data center fleet consuming thousands of megawatts.
The "Behind-the-Meter" Reality: Despite 100% renewable claims, no Virginia hyperscaler currently operates co-located clean energy. All "green" claims rely on offsite Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs), while the physical trend at the meter is pivoting toward on-site gas turbines and diesel backup to ensure firming capacity.
The $1.6B Standoff: A critical fight over $1.6 billion in data center sales tax exemptions remains deadlocked, with a high-stakes special session scheduled for April 23.
